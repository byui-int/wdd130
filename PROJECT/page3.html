Overall Tips!

The main tips that goes for all the sites mentioned is using the list of categories provided by the site, searching by author’s names, searching specific words, or specific titles if you know them. If you know a specific author, say Cindy Roland Anderson, you can look her name up to see what books the site has to offer. In Kindle, Kindle Unlimited and Audible you can scroll down to see other authors that are similar to them. If you search up a title of a book you can also scroll down to see what books are similar to what you currently are looking at. Sometimes books by the same author will show, but most of the time you will see books by other authors that have the same story line or interest.

Kindle & Kindle Unlimited & Audible

For Kindle, Kindle Unlimited, and Audible the category list is quite large. From Arts & Photography to Travel it is well broken down. After you pick your original topic, say Romance, you get into secondary categories. This is where you get to be more specific as to what type of ‘romance’ (or other topic) you are searching for. Action & Adventure, Fantasy, Paranormal, Sports. After you pick a second, more specific category, you are given two more lists to pick from. There is the list of ‘Heroes’ and ‘Themes’. Within these categories you can pick which you would like to be present and which you would not. An example of what the results would look like is, “Romance book with Fantasy with Doctors (heroes) and Vacation (themes)”. Only books with all these items would show up.

OverDrive & Libby

For OverDrive and Libby the same process is available however there are a couple extra steps. Because these sites are different libraries rather than a place where you can buy books you will have additional options to pick from. On OverDrive you can pick to only see books that are available to borrow now rather than seeing books that have a waiting list. You can also pick if you would like to see both audio books and reading books or just one or the other. There are not as many first categories to pick from, but they do make up for it in the secondary categories list. A downside to this site is you only have access to the books that your library has. If they do not own the book it will not be available. On the plus side you can have more than one library on your account. If you have more than one library card with different libraries you can better your chances as finding what you are looking for.
